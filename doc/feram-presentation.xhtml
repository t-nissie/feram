What is feram?

feram is a FAST molecular dynamics (MD) simulator for bulk and thin-film ferroelectrics and relaxors.

Cooling and heating simulation of BaTiO3. Horizontal axis is strain. Difference between first- and second order transition is clear.

Technical features and keywords

  • Fast
    • FFTW library version 3 http://www.fftw.org/
    • Parallelized with OpenMP
    • gfortran on x86_64 (ifort, g95, pgf95)
    • HITACHI SR11000 Super Technical Server
  • Object oriented programming (OOP) in Fortran 2003
  • GNU autotools http://www.gnu.org/software/autoconf/
  • Free software (GPLv3) http://loto.sf.net/feram/
  • Subversion repository at https://loto.svn.sf.net/svnroot/loto/feram/

Perovskite type ferroelectrics ABO3

King-Smith and Vanderbilt: Phys. Rev. B Vol.49 p.5828 (1994): Parametrization of coupling between soft-mode displacement and strains.

Coupling between atomic displacements and strains. After Hashimoto et al.: Jpn. J. Appl. Phys. Vol.43 p.6785 (2004).

Effective Hamiltonian for molecular dynamics simulation

  • Monte Carlo Simulations
    • BaTiO3 bulk - Zhong, Vanderbilt and Rabe: Phys. Rev. B Vol.52 p.6301 (1995).
    • PbTiO3 bulk - Waghmare and Rabe: Phys. Rev. B Vol.55 p.6161 (1997).
  • Molecular dynamics simulations
    • Relaxor - Waghmare, Cockayne and Burton, Ferroelectrics Vol.291, p.187 (2003).
    • Relaxor - Tinte, Burton, Cockayne and Waghmare: Phys. Rev. Lett. Vol.97 p.137601 (2006).
    • BaTiO3 capacitor - Paul, Nishimatsu, Kawazoe and Waghmare: Phys. Rev. Lett. Vol.99 p.077601 (2007) *feram*.

Kinetic energy terms are added to Monte Carlo Hamiltonian Heff=Mdipole*2∑R,αu⋅α2(R)+Macoustic*2∑R,αw⋅α2(R)+Vself({u})+Vdpl({u})+Vshort({u})+Velas,homo(η1,...,η6)+Velas,inho({w})+Vcoup,homo({u},η1,...,η6)+Vcoup,inho({u},{w})

Coarse-graining: reduction of the number of degree of freedom

  • Real perovskite-like system has 15N+6 degree of freedom
    • N unit cells in supercell
    • 5 atoms per unit cell
    • Each atom can move along 3 directions
    • 6 components of strain
  • Simplified model has 6N+6 degree of freedom
    • Define 1 dipole Z*u(R) per unit cell
    • 1 acoustic displacement (Inhomogeneous strain) w(R) per unit cell

Summary for "feram" introduction

  • Molecular dynamics (MD) simulation with first-principles-based effective Hamiltonian
  • New programming and technical methods are employed
  • Perovskite-type ferroelectrics and relaxor
  • Phase transition
  • Thin-film capacitor: perfect and imperfect short-circuited electrodes
  • Nose-Poincare thermostat
  • Evaluate forces in reciprocal-space; k-locality of the force matrix
  • Coarse-graining; reduction of the number of degree of freedom
  • Dielectric constant, permittivity, or susceptibility can be calculated from dipole fluctuation.

---------------------------- Relaxor ----------------------------

Relaxors PbBx'B(1-x)''O3: Pb2+

  • Pb(Sc1/2Nb1/2)O3 (PSN): Sc3+, Nb5+
  • Pb(Sc1/2Ta1/2)O3 (PST): Sc3+, Ta5+
  • Pb(Mg1/3Nb2/3)O3 (PMN:) Mg2+, Nb5+
  • (1-x)Pb(Mg1/3Nb2/3)O3-xPbTiO3 (PMN-xPT): Mg2+, Nb5+, Ti4+
  • Pb(Mg1/3Ta2/3)O3 (PMT): Mg2+, Ta5+
  • Pb(Zn1/3Nb2/3)O3 (PZN): Zn2+, Nb5+

The averaged valence number of B-site ions Bx' and B(1-x)'' is 4+.

Crystal structures of relaxors

(a) Ordered B-site structure of ABx'B(1-x)''O3. In the case of Pb(Sc1/2Nb1/2)O3, Sc and Nb has the NaCl structure. In the case of Pb(Mg1/3Nb2/3)O3, the Na-site is randomly occupied by Mg1/3Nb1/6 and the Cl-site is occupied by Nb1/2. (b) Disordered structure. B-sites is occupied by B' and B(1-x)'' randomly. After C. A. Randall and A. S. Bhalla: Jpn. J. Appl. Phys. Vol. 29 pp.327-333 (1990).
